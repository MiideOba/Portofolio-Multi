About Me

Part 1

Abdul-Quadri Ayomide Al-Ameen, popularly known as Sen. MiideOba, is an indigene of Oke-Ogun, Oyo State, Nigeria, born into the Al-Ameen family on September 19. He began his education at Muslim Association of Nigeria (MAN) Primary School, Okeho, before relocating to Lagos where he completed his primary education at Lad-Ikhlass Private School, Shasha, serving as the Social Prefect. He later graduated from MAN Secondary School, Egbeda, Lagos. He is a recent graduate of Agricultural Extension and Rural Sociology from the prestigious Olabisi Onabanjo University, Ogun State, where he finished with a Second-Class Upper Division. He is a Social Media Manager with proven experience in building online communities, growing brand visibility and creating engaging content across multiple platforms. With a strong background in content strategy, analytics, and storytelling, He specialize in transforming online presence into measurable results. He managed both personal projects and organizational accounts, showing that he can adapt strategies to different audiences while consistently driving growth and engagement. Evidence of Success (Selected Achievements) LinkedIn: Grew my professional account from 19 to 500+ followers in just 2 months, focusing on visibility, storytelling, and professional branding. Twitter (X): Built a personal following of 3,000+, leveraging engagement-driven content and trends. Instagram (Personal): Generated 13,000+ views in 2 months with only 394 followers, showing strong organic reach. TikTok (Personal): Reached 31,000 post views, 1,000 profile visits, and 4,000 likes in 2 months despite only posting occasionally. Facebook (Page): Achieved 920% increase in views within 28 days, 100% increase in engagement, and grew to 3,218 total followers. Organizational Leadership: Co-created and co-managed the Nigerian Association of Agricultural Students (NAAS) OOU social media account. Handled content creation, caption writing, and posting strategy for NAAS and AESAN OOU IG account, building consistency and engagement.

During his years on campus, he was actively involved in student leadership and made a significant mark in service. From his earliest days, he served as Class Representative from 100 to 500 level, later becoming a two-term OOUSU Senator, Coordinator of the Departmental Association, Vice President of NAAS OOU, and eventually the 35th President of the Association. He also served as Vice President of the Leo Club, Ayetoro Campus, and was an active member of the Mental Health Support Initiative (MHSI) and the Muslim Students’ Society of Nigeria (MSSN).

His leadership journey was marked by impactful programs and events, including the NAAS Agro-Summit 2024 featuring Farmer Akin Alabi, the NAAS Ojude Faaji Cultural Festival, Freshers’ Week, the NAAS Super League and 5-aside football competitions, the Provost Cup Tournament, and awareness campaigns such as the War Against Drug Abuse Seminar in partnership with NDLEA.

Part 2

He also initiated welfare projects, tutorial classes, student security advocacy with the Police and DSS, and mobilized fundraising efforts for indigent students. His contributions and dedication to service earned him multiple awards and recognitions. He was honored as the Executive of the Year by NAAS OOU, received the Award of Excellence in Leadership at the OOU Leadership Conference, was named Most Influential Senator by NAAS OOU, and gained recognition for his contributions to student welfare.

He also shared in the recognition of the Clique of the Year (Bat Boys), and though nominated twice as Best Class Rep in the OOU SUG Awards, he narrowly missed the win, yet the nominations reflected his impact. He also holds certifications as a First-Tier Responder in Mental Health (MHSI) and Soft Skills Training (Jobberman Nigeria).

Ayomide is known for his empathetic and selfless leadership style, with many describing him as a servant leader who prioritizes people and impact over titles. His ability to balance leadership responsibilities with academic excellence speaks to his resilience and dedication. Beyond graduation, he continues to grow as an agri-tech enthusiast, volunteer, writer, and public speaker. His professional interests lie in agricultural extension, ICT in agriculture, sustainable agriculture, and human resources development. With strong skills in communication, project coordination, social media management, teamwork, political analysis, and problem-solving, he remains committed to contributing to research, development, and leadership that uplifts communities.
